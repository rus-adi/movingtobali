## Quick take

With toddlers, the “best” area is usually the one that makes **daily life predictable**:

- Short trips (nap windows + traffic don’t mix)
- Easy access to basics (groceries, a clinic, a quiet café/park, a kids activity)
- A home that stays cool enough for naps
- A routine you can repeat even when you’re new

This guide helps you shortlist 2–3 areas for a **test stay** — not “pick the perfect forever neighborhood.”

## What matters most for toddlers

### 1) Commute and nap math
If a typical errand turns into a 45–60 minute ride, it affects everything:
- nap timing
- snack timing
- parent patience
- the ability to say “yes” to small outings

**Rule of thumb:** choose an area where your daily loop (home → groceries → activity → home) is realistically doable.

### 2) Shade, heat, and walkability
You don’t need a “walkable city,” but you do want:
- shaded paths where possible
- places you can pop into with a stroller
- safe, short “outdoor resets” (even 15 minutes matters)

### 3) Medical access and “backup plans”
Most toddler stress comes from “what if” moments:
- fever at night
- falls and bumps
- stomach bugs
- rash / allergy surprises

Choose an area where you can answer: **“What’s our plan if we need help today?”**

### 4) Community + routines
Toddlers thrive when parents have support:
- other families nearby
- playgroups or simple activities
- predictable food + groceries

## A simple shortlist method (10 minutes)

Pick 2–3 areas where you can say “yes” to all three:

1) **We can keep our routine.** (sleep / meals / calm time)  
2) **We can get help quickly.** (clinic + basic services)  
3) **We can leave the house easily.** (short outings without “traffic dread”)

Then do a **test stay** for 10–14 days before you commit to longer-term housing.

## Areas that often fit toddlers (with tradeoffs)

These are *common* toddler-friendly fits. Your exact street matters a lot.

### Sanur
- Often feels calmer and more routine-friendly
- Easier “family loop” day-to-day
- Tradeoff: nightlife is quieter; some families find it “too calm” after a few months  
Explore: [/areas/sanur](/areas/sanur)

### Ubud
- Nature, slower pace, lots of kid-friendly classes and community
- Tradeoff: humidity, rain patterns, and some roads can be slower than you expect  
Explore: [/areas/ubud](/areas/ubud)

### Renon / Denpasar (family base option)
- More “city practical” for appointments and services
- Tradeoff: not a beach/nature vibe; you’ll drive for weekend scenery  
Explore: [/areas/renon](/areas/renon) and [/areas/denpasar](/areas/denpasar)

### Jimbaran / Nusa Dua (resort-adjacent routine)
- Often predictable, quieter, and easier for beach days
- Tradeoff: it can feel “spread out” and car-dependent  
Explore: [/areas/jimbaran](/areas/jimbaran) and [/areas/nusa-dua](/areas/nusa-dua)

## Areas to approach carefully (not “bad,” just plan for reality)

### Canggu / Berawa / Pererenan
These can work beautifully — but traffic and noise can be real.

If you’re considering them, do a test stay and ask:
- “Can we do groceries and one activity without losing the whole day?”
- “Are we okay with scooters around our daily route?”
Explore: [/areas/canggu](/areas/canggu), [/areas/berawa](/areas/berawa), [/areas/pererenan](/areas/pererenan)

### Seminyak / Kerobokan
Great access to restaurants and services, but can feel busy.
Explore: [/areas/seminyak](/areas/seminyak), [/areas/kerobokan](/areas/kerobokan)

## Checklist: toddler test-stay questions

Copy/paste this into your notes:

- Where is the closest clinic we’d actually use?
- How long is groceries → home at 5pm?
- Where do we go for a 30-minute “reset” outside?
- Is our home cool enough for naps?
- Can we find familiar foods quickly?
- What’s our rainy-day activity plan?
