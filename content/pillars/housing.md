## Quick start (the fast version)

- Shortlist **2–3 areas first** (pace + commute + noise matter more than the villa photos): [Areas hub](/areas).
- If you can, do a **short stay**, view places in person, then sign.
- Treat deposits as high-risk moments: verify identity, contract, and what’s included.
- Copy/paste tools:
  - [Lease + deposit checklist](/resources/lease-deposit-checklist)
  - [Lease + deposit questions](/guides/lease-and-deposit-questions-in-bali)
- Do commute tests at real times (morning school run + afternoon return).

## The housing approach that keeps families safe

In our experience, the safest pattern is:

1) **Short stay** (2–14 days)
2) **View in person** (at least 3–6 options)
3) **Verify + negotiate** (clarify what’s included)
4) **Pay deposits only after clarity**

## Step 1: Pick areas by daily life (not by the prettiest listing)

The “right” area isn’t a vibe — it’s daily rhythm:

- **Commute tolerance:** what’s your max daily drive time?
- **Noise:** can your kids sleep if there’s traffic or nightlife nearby?
- **Walkability:** can you do one or two daily tasks on foot?
- **Community:** do you have a realistic path to friends and routines?

## Step 2: Where families find rentals (and how to reduce risk)

Common sources:

- **Agents:** can be helpful for speed, but still verify everything.
- **Facebook groups:** sometimes useful, sometimes risky. Assume listings are inconsistent.
- **Word of mouth:** often the best quality, but harder when you’re new.
- **Online platforms:** useful for a first short stay, not always great for long-term value.

A helpful rule:
If the story changes when you ask basic questions, **walk away**.

---

## Step 3: What to verify before you pay anything

This is where families get burned — so we make it explicit.

Verify:

- **Who owns the property (or who legally represents the owner)**
- **What’s included** (electricity, internet, cleaning, pool/garden)
- **What happens when something breaks** (response time + who pays)
- **Deposit terms** (when it’s returned, what it can be used for)
- **Payment schedule** (monthly/quarterly/yearly)
- **Receipts** (always)

## Step 4: The “family comfort” inspection (quick walkthrough)

When you view a place, do a quick parent scan:

- **Mold + airflow:** check bedrooms and bathrooms.
- **Water pressure:** try showers and sinks.
- **Internet reality:** do a speed test at the times you’ll need it.
- **Noise check:** stand quietly for 60 seconds.
- **Safety basics:** stairs, balconies, pool fencing, gates.

If a villa is beautiful but uncomfortable, it will drain you.

---

## Step 5: Commute is the hidden cost

In Bali, commute isn’t a minor detail. It affects:

- how calm mornings feel,
- whether kids can do after-school activities,
- whether parents can work.

If school is part of your plan, use it as a “pin” on the map and test drive times.

## What we learned the hard way

The biggest housing stress isn’t “finding something.”

It’s paying money before clarity.

If you only follow one rule, follow this:
**Don’t let urgency override verification.**
