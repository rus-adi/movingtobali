## Quick start (the fast version)

- Start with **timeline + length of stay** (2 weeks, 2 months, 12 months). That choice drives everything else.
- Bookmark **Official links** now (this is how you avoid stale advice): [Official links](/official-links).
- If you can, do a **short test stay first**, then decide on longer-term visas once you’ve seen daily life.
- Avoid anyone selling “**guaranteed approval**” or pressuring you to pay today.
- Use a simple tool to compare help: [Visa agent comparison template](/resources/visa-agent-comparison-template).

## The calm way to think about visas

Visa rules are one of the most **changeable** parts of Bali life — which is exactly why this hub is written from experience.

What we aim to do here:
- help you make the *first few decisions* without panic,
- show you the questions that protect families,
- and point you to official portals for anything that changes often.

If you’re unsure about a detail, treat this as your default:
**check the official portal → ask your agent/school → then decide.**

## Step 1: Decide your “first landing” plan

Most stress comes from trying to solve 12 months of visas before you’ve even landed.

A calmer pattern we’ve seen work for families:

1) **Short test stay** (to confirm area + routines)
2) **Staged commitments** (housing + schooling + longer-stay visa)
3) **Re-check official requirements** before you pay or submit

## Step 3: When an agent is worth it

An agent can be helpful when:
- you’re aiming for a longer stay,
- your situation is complex (family structure, timing, documentation),
- you want someone to handle forms/checklists while you focus on family logistics.

An agent is *not* a guarantee.
If someone promises certainty, treat that as a red flag.

## Step 4: The due‑diligence questions (copy/paste)

These are simple questions that protect families:

- **What visa type are you recommending and why?** (Ask for reasoning, not just a price.)
- **What documents will you need from us?** (Get the checklist in writing.)
- **What are the total fees and what’s included?** (Avoid surprise add-ons.)
- **What’s the timeline range (best / typical / worst case)?**
- **What happens if something changes mid-process?**
- **How do you communicate?** (WhatsApp is common — ask expected response times.)
- **Do you provide receipts / proof of submission?**

Then compare answers side-by-side:
- [Visa agent comparison template](/resources/visa-agent-comparison-template)

## Step 5: Visa scam red flags (what to watch out for)

We keep this practical and non-dramatic — just patterns families can avoid:

- “Guaranteed approval” or “no risk” language
- Pressure to pay immediately (especially from overseas)
- Vague pricing (“depends later”) without a written breakdown
- No written checklist, no receipts, no proof of submission
- Blaming you for asking basic questions

Full guide:
- [Visa scam red flags](/guides/visa-scam-red-flags)

## What we learned the hard way

The biggest stress reducer wasn’t “perfect visa knowledge.”

It was **staged commitments**:
- commit to a short stay first,
- confirm area + routines,
- then choose longer-term options with more confidence.
